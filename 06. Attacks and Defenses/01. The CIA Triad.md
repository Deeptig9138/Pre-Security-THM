# 🛡️ The CIA Triad 

## 📘 Overview

The **CIA Triad** is one of the most important concepts in cybersecurity.

Now that we understand computers, operating systems, software, networks, and the web, we can begin exploring what cybersecurity actually protects.

Cybersecurity is not only about stopping hackers or installing security tools. It is about protecting digital information and ensuring three critical security properties remain intact:

- **Confidentiality**
- **Integrity**
- **Availability**

Together, these three pillars form the **CIA Triad**.

> 💡 **Core Idea:**  
> Cybersecurity exists to protect the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of digital information.

## 🧩 Task 2: Understanding the CIA Triad

### 🧠 What is the CIA Triad?

The CIA Triad is a framework that defines the three fundamental goals of cybersecurity.

### 🔒 Confidentiality

#### 🧠 Definition

Confidentiality ensures that information is accessible only to authorized individuals.

Unauthorized users should not be able to view sensitive data.

#### 🚶 Real-World Example

Imagine having a private conversation with a friend.

Someone secretly listens to the conversation and later uses that information against you.

The information was supposed to remain private but was accessed by an unauthorized person.

This is a failure of confidentiality.

#### 💻 Digital Example

Suppose you connect to a public Wi-Fi network at a coffee shop.

While logging into a social media account, an attacker intercepts your credentials.

The attacker now has access to information they should never have seen.

Confidentiality has been compromised.

#### 🛠️ Common Methods to Protect Confidentiality

Examples include:

- Encryption
- Passwords
- Access controls
- Authentication systems

#### 📋 Confidentiality Examples

| Situation | Confidentiality Achieved? |
|------------|--------------------------|
| Gmail password written on sticky notes | ❌ No |
| Employees access only documents required for work | ✅ Yes |
| Personal document exposed on the internet | ❌ No |

#### 🎯 Key Insight

> Confidentiality protects information from unauthorized access.

### 🔐 Integrity

#### 🧠 Definition

Integrity ensures that information cannot be modified without authorization.

Data should remain accurate, trustworthy, and unchanged unless properly approved.

#### 🚶 Real-World Example

Imagine your teacher awards you a high exam score.

Before the score is submitted, someone secretly changes it.

The information is no longer accurate.

Integrity has been violated.

#### 💻 Digital Example

Suppose you transfer money through online banking.

An attacker intercepts the transaction and changes the destination account.

The money is sent somewhere else.

The transaction data was altered.

Integrity has been compromised.

#### 🛠️ Common Methods to Protect Integrity

Examples include:

- Hashing
- Digital signatures
- Validation checks
- Access controls

#### 📋 Integrity Examples

| Situation | Integrity Achieved? |
|------------|--------------------|
| Data changed through approved process | ✅ Yes |
| Student attendance modified after being locked | ❌ No |
| Product price altered before checkout | ❌ No |

#### 🎯 Key Insight

> Integrity protects information from unauthorized modification.

### ⚡ Availability

#### 🧠 Definition

Availability ensures that information and services remain accessible when authorized users need them.

Information is only useful if it can actually be accessed.

#### 🚶 Real-World Example

Imagine depositing money in a bank.

Your money is secure, but when you need it, the bank is closed due to a power outage.

Your funds still exist, but you cannot access them.

Availability has been affected.

#### 💻 Digital Example

Suppose attackers flood a website with massive amounts of traffic.

The website becomes overloaded and crashes.

No information was stolen.

No information was modified.

However, users cannot access the service.

Availability has been compromised.

#### 🛠️ Common Methods to Protect Availability

Examples include:

- Backup systems
- Redundant infrastructure
- Load balancing
- Disaster recovery plans
- Power generators

#### 📋 Availability Examples

| Situation | Availability Achieved? |
|------------|-----------------------|
| Critical service disrupted by software installation | ❌ No |
| Company website offline during business hours | ❌ No |
| Employees can access systems during work hours | ✅ Yes |

#### 🎯 Key Insight

> Availability ensures information and services remain accessible when needed.

### 📊 CIA Triad Summary

| Pillar | Purpose |
|----------|----------|
| Confidentiality | Prevent unauthorized access |
| Integrity | Prevent unauthorized modification |
| Availability | Ensure access when needed |

## 🧩 Task 3: The Security Mindset

### 🧠 Beyond Definitions

The CIA Triad is more than a collection of terms.

It represents the way cybersecurity professionals think about security problems.

This approach is called a:

```text
Security Mindset
```

### 🎯 How Security Professionals Think

When a security incident occurs, professionals often ask:

#### Was sensitive information exposed?

If yes:

```text
Confidentiality impacted
```

#### Was information altered?

If yes:

```text
Integrity impacted
```

#### Were systems unavailable?

If yes:

```text
Availability impacted
```

### 🧠 Why This Matters

Understanding which pillar is affected helps determine:

- The severity of an incident
- The potential consequences
- The appropriate response

### 🎮 Hands-On Scenario

During a cybersecurity workshop, participants are presented with various security incidents.

Their task is to identify which CIA pillar is affected most by each incident.

Examples include:

- Exposed credentials
- Altered records
- Offline services

The goal is to develop a security-focused way of thinking.
